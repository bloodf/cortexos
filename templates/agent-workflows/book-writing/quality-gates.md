# Book Writing Quality Gates

## Gate 1 — Draft Complete

Owner: book-author

Pass when:

- The chapter answers the issue brief.
- The draft has a clear beginning, middle, and end.
- Open questions are listed explicitly.
- Unsupported factual claims are marked.

Block when:

- The chapter is mostly outline or notes.
- The chapter contradicts the approved thesis.
- The author fabricated sources, quotes, or examples.

## Gate 2 — Editorial Ready

Owner: book-editor

Pass when:

- Structure supports reader comprehension.
- Voice matches the style guide.
- Repetition and generic filler are removed.
- Transitions connect the chapter to surrounding material.

Block when:

- Meaning changed without explanation.
- Voice is flattened into generic prose.
- Major continuity issues remain.

## Gate 3 — Review Passed

Owner: book-reviewer

Pass when:

- Acceptance criteria are met.
- Claims are supportable or flagged.
- Examples are relevant and specific.
- Reader-impacting issues are resolved.

Block when:

- The chapter misleads the reader.
- Required sections are missing.
- Reviewer cannot verify critical claims.

## Gate 4 — Evaluation Passed

Owner: book-evaluator

Score each dimension from 1-5:

- Promise fit
- Reader clarity
- Specificity
- Continuity
- Trust
- Voice

Pass when:

- All dimensions are 3 or higher.
- Trust is 4 or higher for factual chapters.
- The evaluator can cite evidence for each score.

Block when:

- Any dimension is below threshold.
- The chapter is fluent but does not deliver the promise.
- Evaluation evidence is missing.

## Gate 5 — Translation Accepted

Owner: book-translator plus reviewer when required

Pass when:

- Meaning is preserved.
- Glossary terms are consistent.
- Markdown structure is intact.
- Locale-specific adaptations are documented.

Block when:

- Translation summarizes instead of translating.
- Links, code blocks, or notes are dropped.
- Sensitive localization decisions were made without review.
